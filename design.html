Our design

Justification

Our Wireframe!

We first researched what brands that offer similiar product and service (clothes and online shopping) do on their website.



We decided to use a basic layout that is easy to understand at first glance and has a frame view: header and a footer. Throughout the website a few elements are always present: the header, which contains the name of the brand; the navigation bar with interactive tabs for browsing the website, as well as the logo; and a footer with a brief explanation of the brand and links to our social media accounts and email address.


Our first draft was created on paint and after receiving feedback by the expert for this course we created a more detailed draft on photoshop. That draft was later transferred into a low fidelity digital prototype made with Adobe XD; also used for our user testings.

Our Fonts!

We decide to use a more formal and stronger font for our titles and important pieces of text as it attracts the attention of the reader immediately. For our standard text we chose a soft yet still formal font - it is both readable and easy on the eyes.

Our color palette!

We choose pastel colors that have soft shades and are gender neutral.

Our Prototype and User Testing!

Our main concern in regards to creating our website was to make sure the layout was easy to use and understandable for our target group. To ensure this we conducted three user testing interviews with our low-fidelity prototype (created with Adobe XD).

The interviews followed the same scenario:
1. What do you think of the layout?
2. How do you find the navigation process around the site?
3.Can you purchase a product? (no instruction were further provided after giving the task)
4. Additional remarks.

All of our testers found our layout simple and easy to understand, which is the reason why we kept it for our final execution. The tester’s were all successful in going around the website and pointed out that they like how the navigation is everywhere on the website and it contains everything that the website offers.
They all managed to “purchase a product” and were satisfied with the easiness of the process.
In conclusion, based on our results from the user testing interviews we kept our initial layout and flowchart, but we made the edges of the boxes (containers) a bit softer by either removing the border color or using a padding in the styling. We also took into account their suggestions for our content mainly placing visuals and being more transparent about our products.
